## EDU-REQ-0104: Progress Reporting Shows Development Trajectories

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=Progress Reporting System, Severity=Major, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt shall generate progress reports showing multidimensional learning trajectories to students, parents, and educators in audience-appropriate formats.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Reports must show learning as a trajectory (how student is improving), not snapshots (test score today)
- **Observable Behaviors:** Reports distinguish learning progress from performance, show trends, include multiple dimensions
- **Compliance Criteria:** Reports available to all stakeholders; each audience gets appropriately formatted report
- **Failure Mode:** Reports show only test scores → violates transparency and multidimensional tracking

## EDU-REQ-0105: Curriculum Design Ensures Coherent Long-Term Development

**METADATA:** Type=Process, Owner=Curriculum Design/Content Team, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt's curriculum shall be designed with explicit prerequisites, progressive depth, and pacing that allows Understanding to develop before advancing.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Curriculum is a scaffold, not a random collection; foundational concepts revisited at increasing depth
- **Invariants:** Must never prioritize coverage over depth; must never skip prerequisite concepts
- **Observable Behaviors:** Curriculum map shows prerequisite relationships; later topics explicitly reference earlier ones
- **Compliance Criteria:** Prerequisite chains documented; pacing allows 2-3 weeks per topic minimum

## EDU-REQ-0106: Student Goal-Setting Facilitates Explicit Conversation

**METADATA:** Type=Process, Owner=Student Goal-Setting System, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt shall facilitate conversations that distinguish student's own educational goals, exam requirements, and Adapt's recommendations, recording these distinctions to inform prioritization.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Students should understand why Adapt recommends what it does; goals should drive decisions transparently
- **Observable Behaviors:** Goals are documented; student sees how each goal affects scheduling; goals are revisited periodically
- **Failure Mode:** Student confusion about why Adapt recommends coverage/spacing/challenge

---

## EDU-REQ-0107: Preference Learning Respects Student Preferences

**METADATA:** Type=Process, Owner=Preference Learning System, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt shall learn and respect student preferences when pedagogically permissible, and transparently explain when preferences conflict with educational requirements.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Students have preferences (problem types, explanation modality, timing); respect them when learning isn't compromised
- **Observable Behaviors:** System learns preferences from behavior; incorporates them into problem selection when possible
- **Compliance Criteria:** Preference conflicts are logged with explanation to student
- **Failure Mode:** System ignores all preferences → reduces engagement; system respects all preferences → compromises learning

---

## EDU-REQ-0108: Conflict Resolution Identifies and Logs Stakeholder Tensions

**METADATA:** Type=Process, Owner=PRE, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
When educational values conflict (e.g., challenge vs. confidence, coverage vs. depth), Adapt shall identify the conflict, log it with reasoning, and resolve it by prioritizing: student wellbeing > long-term learning > exam performance > coverage.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Conflicts are inevitable; handle them transparently, not silently
- **Observable Behaviors:** Conflict log documents tensions and resolutions; resolutions are auditable
- **Priority Order:** Wellbeing > Learning > Performance > Coverage (never harm wellbeing for exam performance)

---

## EDU-REQ-0109: Stakeholder Communication Generates Audience-Specific Reports

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=Communication/Reporting System, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt shall generate communication tailored to different stakeholders: students see motivation and learning progress, parents see predicted exam grades with learning context, educators see detailed diagnostics.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Different audiences need different information in different formats
- **Observable Behaviors:** Student reports emphasize growth and areas for improvement; parent reports include exam predictions; educator reports include error patterns and misconceptions
- **Compliance Criteria:** All reports in clear, accessible language; no jargon without explanation

---

## EDU-REQ-0110: Student Advocacy Defaults to Student Preference with Justification

**METADATA:** Type=Governance, Owner=PRE/Escalation, Severity=Moderate, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
On genuine decisions, Adapt shall default to student preference; on essential decisions (spacing, challenge calibration), Adapt shall maintain expert guidance but explain conflicts transparently and escalate to humans if disagreement persists.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Respect student agency while protecting learning
- **Observable Behaviors:** System distinguishes genuine vs. essential decisions; overrides documented; escalations tracked
- **Compliance Criteria:** All student overrides explained; escalations resolved by human review within 24 hours
