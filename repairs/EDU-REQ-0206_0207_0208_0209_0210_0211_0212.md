## EDU-REQ-0206: Spacing System Maintains Learning Event Records

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=SIS, Severity=Critical, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt's Student Intelligence System shall maintain persistent, timestamped records of all Learning Events including problem presented, student response, correctness status, and attempt timestamp, enabling calculation of optimal Retrieval opportunities based on Spacing principles.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Spacing depends on knowing precisely when learning occurred and what was retained. Records are foundational.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Complete event history retrievable; no events lost; timestamps precise; data enables spacing calculations
- **Failure Mode:** Incomplete records → unreliable spacing schedules; lost records → breaks spacing chain

**NOTE:** Original requirement "Spacing System Tracks and Schedules Retrieval" contains multiple obligations (track records, estimate retention, calculate schedules, schedule retrieval, adapt schedules). This requirement covers the "maintain records" obligation. Remaining obligations split into EDU-REQ-0207, EDU-REQ-0208, EDU-REQ-0209 (new UIDs created).

Adapt's Spacing System shall calculate optimal timing for retrieval opportunities based on Spacing principles and Retention estimates, and automatically schedule retrieval activities accordingly.

## EDU-REQ-0208: Retrieval Practice Included in All Problem Sequences

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=PRE, Severity=Critical, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
All learning problem sequences shall include Retrieval Practice of previously learned concepts, interleaved with new content, constituting a significant proportion of all presented problems and calibrated to challenge without overwhelming.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Learning isn't drilling new content; it's retrieving and strengthening old content. Every sequence must do both.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Problem sequences include retrieval; retrieval interleaved (not blocked); success rates calibrated
- **Invariants:** Must never defer retrieval practice because student prefers new content; must never skip retrieval because "student succeeded before"

## EDU-REQ-0209: Interleaving Included in All Practice Activities

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=Problem Generation System, Severity=Critical, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
All practice problem sets shall include problems from multiple topics and types interleaved rather than blocked by topic, with interleaving ratios at least 30% non-primary topics, and students educated about why interleaving produces better long-term learning despite feeling harder.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Blocked practice (all math problems, then all grammar) feels easier but produces weaker transfer. Interleaving feels harder but produces durable learning.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Practice sets analyzed for interleaving; students receive metacognitive instruction on interleaving benefits
- **Compliance Criteria:** Interleaving ratio ≥30%; metacognitive instruction documented; student understanding tracked

## EDU-REQ-0210: Error Analysis Diagnoses Misconceptions

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=PRE, Severity=Major, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
When a student makes an error, Adapt shall analyze the error to diagnose underlying misconceptions, track patterns across problem instances to identify systematic errors, and recommend targeted interventions based on diagnosis.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Errors reveal thinking. An error might indicate: misunderstanding the concept, careless mistake, or misconception (coherent but wrong mental model). Distinguish them.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Errors categorized by type; patterns tracked; misconceptions identified; interventions recommended
- **Compliance Criteria:** Error analysis documented; misconception diagnoses auditable; interventions targeted to specific misconceptions

## EDU-REQ-0211: Elaboration Connects to Prior Knowledge

**METADATA:** Type=Process, Owner=Explanation Generation System, Severity=Major, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt's explanations shall explicitly connect new concepts to related prior knowledge, helping students understand how new learning relates to existing knowledge, and provide examples demonstrating application across varied contexts to support Transfer.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Connection to prior knowledge is how new information becomes integrated, not isolated. Make the connection explicit.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Explanations reference prior concepts; examples show varied contexts; students understand relationship to prior knowledge

## EDU-REQ-0212: Feedback System Provides Corrective, Targeted Feedback

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=PRE, Severity=Major, Stability=Core

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Adapt's Feedback System shall provide corrective, diagnostically targeted feedback identifying what was incorrect, why it was incorrect, and how to correct it, with timing calibrated to learning goal (immediate for procedural learning, delayed for conceptual understanding).

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Feedback that just says "correct" or "incorrect" teaches nothing. Corrective feedback explains the error.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Feedback is corrective (not just evaluative); diagnostically targeted; timing varies by goal
- **Compliance Criteria:** Feedback addressing identified misconceptions; timing appropriate to goal; student understanding improves

## EDU-REQ-0213: Transfer Problems Deliberately Included in Problem Sets

**METADATA:** Type=Outcome, Owner=Problem Generation System, Severity=Major, Stability=Stable

**CONSTITUTIONAL OBLIGATION:**
Problem sets shall include Transfer problems requiring application in novel contexts, including both Near Transfer and Far Transfer problems, calibrated to challenge without overwhelming, with students educated that Transfer difficulty reflects deeper learning rather than failure.

**KEY SECTIONS:**
- **Intent:** Transfer doesn't happen by accident. It requires deliberate practice in new contexts. Include transfer problems from the start.
- **Observable Behaviors:** Problem sets include transfer problems; contexts deliberately novel; student misconceptions about transfer difficulty addressed
- **Compliance Criteria:** Transfer problem ratios tracked; contexts verified as novel; student metacognition about transfer documented

**NOTE:** Original requirement "Retrieval Practice Included in All Problem Sequences" and subsequent requirements contained multiple obligations. Splitting created new UIDs as appropriate.
